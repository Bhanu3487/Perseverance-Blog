Inspirational Story

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," exemplified perseverance throughout his life. Born into a humble family, he faced financial constraints but never let them hinder his education. He worked tirelessly, balancing his studies with part-time jobs. Despite initial failures, Kalam's persistence shone through when he joined ISRO and led the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit.


One of his most remarkable achievements came after the failure of the Agni missile test in 1989. Undeterred by setbacks and criticism, Kalam motivated his team, leading to a successful test in 1992. His unwavering determination and belief in India's scientific potential were pivotal in advancing the country's missile and space programs. Perseverance

Perseverance is the steadfastness in doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success.

We, the Explorers group, have observed that many among us, including our peers, struggle to achieve our goals. A significant factor contributing to this is a lack of persistence. We often give up too easily and tend to back off without making another attempt.

Why We Struggle with Perseverance

  • 1. Low Confidence
  • 2. Lack of Motivation
  • 3. Fear of Failure
  • 4. Distractions

How to Achieve Your Ultimate Goal

  • 1.

    Write Down Your Top-Level Goal
    Clearly define your ultimate goal. It should be specific and not vague. For example, instead of saying, "I want to be fit," say, "I want to run a marathon in six months." Learn more about SMART goals.

  • 2.

    Make a Pyramid of Goals
    Visualize your goals as a pyramid rather than a to-do list. The top of the pyramid is your ultimate goal, and the lower levels are the mid-level and low-level goals that support it."

  • 3.

    Tasks are Low-Level Goals
    Break down your mid-level goals into smaller, manageable tasks. Each task you complete is a step towards achieving your higher-level goals.

  • 4.

    Overcome Setbacks with Analysis
    When you encounter setbacks, analyze what went wrong and why. Use this understanding to adjust your approach and keep moving forward."

  • 5.

    Reevaluation
    Periodically reevaluate your goals and progress. This helps ensure that you are on the right track and allows you to make necessary adjustments.

  • 6.

    Take Rest
    Allow yourself to take breaks and rest. Rest is crucial for maintaining long-term productivity and preventing burnout. Learn more about Rewarding yourself

SMART Goals

S

Specific

Clearly define what you want to achieve.

M

Measurable

Establish criteria to measure your progress.

A

Attainable

Set realistic goals that you can achieve.

R

Relavent

Ensure the goal is important to you and aligns with your broader objectives.

T

Time-bound

Set a deadline for achieving your goal.

Steps to Reward Yourself

Step 1

Identify a task or goal you want to accomplish.

Step 2

Break the task into manageable steps and set a timeline.

Step 3

Choose a meaningful reward for completing the task.

Step 4

Stay focused and motivated as you work towards your goal.

Step 5

Celebrate your achievement and enjoy your reward!
